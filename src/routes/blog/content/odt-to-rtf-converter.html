Why convert to RTF?

  • Universal — Opens in Word, WordPad, TextEdit, and more
  • No special software — Built-in apps can read RTF
  • Lightweight — Smaller than complex formats
  • Safe — No macros or scripts

How to convert

  1. Open TinyUtils Document Converter
  2. Upload your .odt file
  3. Choose RTF format
  4. Click Convert
  5. Share the .rtf file

Opens everywhere

  • Windows: Word, WordPad, LibreOffice
  • Mac: TextEdit, Pages, Word
  • Linux: LibreOffice, AbiWord
  • Mobile: Most document apps

What's preserved

  • Text content and paragraphs
  • Basic formatting (bold, italic, underline)
  • Font styles and sizes
  • Simple tables
  • Lists (bulleted and numbered)

When to use RTF

Cross-platform sharing

Sharing with someone who doesn't have LibreOffice? RTF opens without any special software.

Legal or business documents

When you need maximum compatibility and don't need advanced features like tracked changes.

Email attachments

RTF files pass through email filters better than some other document formats.

FAQ

Is RTF as good as DOCX?

RTF has fewer features but more compatibility. For simple documents, you won't notice the difference.

What about images?

Basic images are supported. Complex graphics may need adjustment after conversion.

Can I edit the RTF and convert back?

Yes, RTF is fully editable. You can convert back to ODT or any other format.
