PNG is great for graphics with transparency, but terrible for photos. If you have a PNG photo (screenshot, export from app), converting to JPG can cut file size by 80% or more.

TL;DR

  • Open TinyUtils Image Compressor
  • Drop your PNG file
  • Select JPG/JPEG output
  • Set quality (85% is usually perfect)
  • Download the smaller JPG

When to convert PNG to JPG

  • Photos — Any photographic image should be JPG
  • Screenshots with photos — If it's mostly photographic content
  • Large images — When file size matters more than perfect quality
  • Email attachments — JPG plays nicer with email size limits
  • Social media — Platforms often recompress to JPG anyway

When NOT to convert

  • Transparency needed — JPG doesn't support transparency
  • Text/UI screenshots — JPG artifacts ruin sharp text
  • Logos and icons — Keep as PNG or SVG
  • Graphics with solid colors — PNG compresses these better

How to convert

  1. Go to TinyUtils Image Compressor
  2. Drop your PNG (or multiple PNGs)
  3. Choose JPG as output
  4. Set quality slider (default 85% is good)
  5. Click Convert
  6. Download individually or as ZIP

What happens to transparency?

JPG doesn't support transparency. Transparent areas become white by default. If you need transparency, use PNG to WebP instead.

FAQ

Will I lose quality?

Yes, but at 85%+ quality, it's usually imperceptible for photos. Text and sharp edges may show artifacts.

Can I convert back to PNG?

Technically yes, but you won't recover the lost quality. Keep your original.

Is WebP better than JPG?

For web use, yes. WebP is smaller at the same quality. But JPG has better compatibility with older software and some social platforms.
