LaTeX is the gold standard for academic and scientific documents. But to share your work, you need PDF. Here's how to convert LaTeX to PDF.

TL;DR

  • Open TinyUtils Document Converter
  • Upload your .tex file
  • Select PDF as output
  • Download the compiled PDF

Why convert LaTeX to PDF?

  • Sharing — Reviewers and readers expect PDF
  • Submission — Journals require PDF uploads
  • Printing — PDF is print-ready
  • No LaTeX install — Compile without installing TeX Live

What compiles

  • Text and formatting — Headings, paragraphs, emphasis
  • Math equations — Rendered beautifully
  • Tables — LaTeX table environments
  • References — Bibliography (if BibTeX included)
  • Figures — Included images

How to convert

  1. Go to TinyUtils Document Converter
  2. Upload your .tex file
  3. Select PDF from output options
  4. Click Convert
  5. Download the PDF

Package support

Common LaTeX packages are supported. Very specialized packages may not be available. For complex documents with custom packages, a full TeX installation is better.

Including images

If your .tex references images, upload everything as a ZIP. The converter will find and include the images.

FAQ

What LaTeX engine is used?

pdfLaTeX for most documents. XeLaTeX features may not be fully supported.

What about BibTeX references?

Include your .bib file. The converter processes bibliography entries.

Can I compile multi-file projects?

Upload as a ZIP with all .tex files, images, and resources. The main .tex file should be named clearly.
