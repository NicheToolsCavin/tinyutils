Why RTF?

  • Universal — Opens in Word, WordPad, TextEdit, LibreOffice, Pages
  • No vendor lock-in — Not tied to any specific application
  • Lightweight — Smaller than DOCX for simple documents
  • Legacy support — Works with older software

Simple documents

For text-heavy documents without complex layouts, RTF is ideal.

FAQ

Is RTF outdated?

It's old but not obsolete. RTF is still the most universally supported rich text format. Perfect for compatibility.

Can RTF include images?

Yes, but RTF handles images less efficiently than modern formats. For image-heavy documents, consider DOCX or PDF.

RTF vs TXT — what's the difference?

TXT is plain text with no formatting. RTF preserves bold, italic, fonts, and colors.
