Got a folder full of images? Compressing them one by one is tedious. Here's how to batch compress multiple images at once.

TL;DR

  • Open TinyUtils Image Compressor
  • Drop all your images (JPG, PNG, WebP, HEIC)
  • Set quality and format options
  • Click Compress
  • Download as ZIP

Why batch compress?

  • Website launches — Optimize all images before going live
  • Email attachments — Fit under size limits
  • Storage — Reclaim disk space
  • Performance — Faster page loads

How to batch compress

  1. Go to TinyUtils Image Compressor
  2. Drag and drop multiple files (or select a folder)
  3. Choose output format (keep original, or convert all to WebP)
  4. Set quality level (85% recommended for photos)
  5. Click Compress All
  6. Download the ZIP containing all compressed images

Supported formats

  • Input: JPG, JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF, HEIC, HEIF
  • Output: JPG, PNG, WebP (or keep original format)

Recommended settings

Use case Format Quality
Website photos WebP 80-85%
Email attachments JPG 75-80%
Graphics/logos PNG N/A (lossless)
Maximum savings WebP 70-75%

Privacy

All processing happens in your browser. Images are never uploaded to a server. Your files stay on your device.

Limits

Browser-based processing handles most typical batches. For thousands of very large images, a desktop tool may be more practical.

FAQ

How many images can I process at once?

Depends on your device. Most can handle 50-100 images easily. Processing happens in parallel.

Will file names be preserved?

Yes. The ZIP contains files with original names (extension may change if you convert formats).

Can I resize while compressing?

Yes. Set max dimensions and images larger than that will be scaled down.
