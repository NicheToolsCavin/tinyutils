LibreOffice saves in ODT format, but the business world runs on Word. When you need to share a document with someone using Microsoft Office, convert ODT to DOCX.

TL;DR

  • Open TinyUtils Document Converter
  • Upload your .odt file
  • Select DOCX as output
  • Download the Word document

Why convert ODT to DOCX?

  • Collaboration — Colleagues use Microsoft Word
  • Compatibility — DOCX opens natively in Word
  • Client requests — They asked for a Word document
  • Track Changes — Use Word's review features

What's preserved

  • Text formatting — Bold, italic, colors, fonts
  • Headings — Mapped to Word heading styles
  • Lists — Bullets and numbers
  • Tables — Structure and formatting
  • Images — Embedded graphics
  • Links — Hyperlinks work

How to convert

  1. Go to TinyUtils Document Converter
  2. Upload your .odt file
  3. Select DOCX from output options
  4. Click Convert
  5. Download the .docx file

Layout considerations

Most layouts convert well. Complex page setups (multiple columns, text boxes) may need minor adjustments after conversion.

Multiple files

Upload multiple ODT files and get a ZIP of DOCX files.

FAQ

Why not just open ODT in Word?

Word can open ODT, but formatting often breaks. Converting first gives cleaner results.

What about macros?

LibreOffice macros don't transfer to Word. Document content converts, but automation doesn't.

Can I edit the DOCX afterward?

Yes, it's a fully editable Word document. That's the point.
