Understanding EPUB and PDF

What is EPUB?

EPUB (Electronic Publication) is the open ebook standard. Its defining characteristic is reflowable text—content that adapts to any screen size, orientation, and reader preferences. EPUB readers let users adjust fonts, sizes, margins, and themes. This flexibility makes EPUB perfect for e-readers but means there are no fixed pages. The same book might be 300 pages on one device and 500 on another, depending on settings.

EPUB uses web technologies internally (XHTML and CSS), which enables this flexibility but also means EPUB lacks the concept of a "page" in the traditional sense.

What is PDF?

PDF (Portable Document Format) is the opposite philosophy. PDFs have fixed pages with exact dimensions. Content stays precisely where it was placed. A PDF looks identical whether viewed on a phone, printed on paper, or displayed on a cinema screen. This consistency makes PDF the standard for printing, professional documents, and anything requiring exact visual reproduction.

The tradeoff: PDFs don't adapt. The pages are the pages. On small screens, you zoom and scroll. But for printing and fixed-layout needs, this is exactly right.

Why Convert EPUB to PDF?

1. Printing

You can't print reflowable content directly—printers need fixed pages. Converting EPUB to PDF creates printable pages with consistent appearance. Print your ebook for physical reading, annotations, or sharing.

2. Universal Sharing

Not everyone has an EPUB reader, but most people can open a PDF. When you’re sharing with someone who doesn’t use e-reader apps, PDF is usually the path of least resistance.

3. Professional Review

Proofreading and editing often happen on PDF. Converting EPUB to PDF enables markup with professional PDF annotation tools—highlighting, comments, strikethroughs.

4. Archival

PDF/A is an ISO-standardized profile aimed at long-term document preservation. Converting an ebook to PDF can give you a stable copy you can keep for later (and back up like anything else you care about).

5. Visual Consistency

Sometimes you need the content to look the same everywhere. PDF helps because the pages are fixed: there’s no reflow, and fewer surprises from reader settings.

6. Reference Copies

For books you reference frequently, a PDF version with page numbers enables consistent citations. "Page 47" in PDF means the same thing to everyone.

What Converts from EPUB to PDF

The conversion preserves content while "freezing" the layout:

  • Text content: All chapters, sections, and text
  • Formatting: Bold, italic, headings, paragraphs
  • Images: Cover image and inline images
  • Links: Hyperlinks (internal and external) become clickable in PDF
  • Table of contents: Becomes PDF bookmarks for navigation
  • Lists: Bulleted and numbered lists
  • Tables: Table structure preserved

Understanding the Fundamental Difference

This is crucial: converting to PDF "freezes" the layout at a specific page size. The reflowable nature of EPUB is lost—that's the point. You gain fixed pages and consistent appearance, but you lose the flexibility of adjustable fonts and screen adaptation.

The conversion chooses sensible defaults for page size (typically US Letter or A4), margins, and fonts. The result is a readable book-formatted PDF, but it's fundamentally different from the EPUB experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will the PDF look like my Kindle display?

The content is the same, but the layout differs. EPUB adjusts to screens and reader settings; PDF has fixed pages. They're fundamentally different viewing experiences for the same content.

What about DRM-protected EPUBs?

DRM-protected ebooks cannot be converted—the DRM prevents it. The file must be DRM-free for conversion to work. Removing DRM may have legal implications depending on your jurisdiction.

Can I convert back to EPUB?

Yes, but PDF to EPUB conversion is imperfect since you're trying to make fixed content reflowable again. Always keep your original EPUB if you might need the reflowable format.

What page size is used?

The converter uses standard page sizes suitable for most printing and viewing. For specific size requirements, you may need to adjust the PDF afterward.

Are images preserved?

Yes—cover images and inline images from the EPUB appear in the PDF at appropriate sizes and positions.

What about the table of contents?

The EPUB's table of contents converts to PDF bookmarks, providing clickable navigation in PDF readers.

What's the maximum file size?

The converter handles EPUB files up to 50MB, which covers essentially all ebooks. Very long books with many images may take slightly longer to process.

When to Keep EPUB

Not every situation calls for PDF:

  • E-reader usage: If you're reading on an e-reader, EPUB is the right format.
  • Adjustability: If you need to change fonts or sizes, keep the EPUB.
  • Phone/tablet reading: EPUB's reflowing works better on small screens.
  • Accessibility: EPUB's adaptability serves accessibility needs better than fixed PDF.
