TL;DR

Why convert EPUB to PDF?

  • Printing — PDF has fixed pages; EPUB reflows
  • Sharing — Everyone can open a PDF
  • Annotation — PDF markup tools work better
  • Archive — PDF/A is a preservation format
  • Consistency — Same appearance on every device

What converts

  • Text content — All chapters and sections
  • Formatting — Bold, italic, headings
  • Images — Cover and inline images
  • Links — Internal and external links
  • Table of contents — Navigable in PDF

EPUB vs PDF: the difference

EPUB is reflowable — text adjusts to screen size. PDF is fixed — pages look the same everywhere. Converting to PDF "freezes" the layout at a specific page size.

How to convert

  1. Go to TinyUtils Document Converter
  2. Upload your .epub file
  3. Select PDF from output options
  4. Click Convert
  5. Download the PDF

Page layout

The converter uses sensible defaults for page size and margins. For custom layouts, you may need post-processing in a PDF editor.

FAQ

Will the PDF look like my Kindle display?

Similar content, but layout differs. EPUB adjusts to screens; PDF has fixed pages.

What about DRM-protected EPUBs?

DRM-protected files can't be converted. You need to remove DRM first (check local laws).

Can I convert back to EPUB?

Yes, but PDF to EPUB is imperfect. Keep your original EPUB.
