Why convert RTF to PDF?

  • Universal viewing — PDFs open everywhere
  • Print-ready — Consistent layout across printers
  • Professional appearance — PDF is the business standard
  • Archive — PDF/A is a preservation format

What's preserved

  • Text formatting — Bold, italic, fonts, colors
  • Paragraphs — Alignment and spacing
  • Tables — Basic table structures
  • Images — Embedded graphics

Legacy documents

RTF files from older software (WordPad, older Word versions) convert well. The format is simple enough that most content transfers cleanly.

FAQ

Why is RTF still used?

RTF is a lowest-common-denominator format. It works across all word processors without compatibility issues. Some legal and government systems still use it.

Is RTF to PDF lossless?

For most documents, yes. Complex layouts may have minor differences.

Can I convert back to RTF?

Yes, but you'd lose PDF-specific features. Better to keep the original RTF.
