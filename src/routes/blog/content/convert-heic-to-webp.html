HEIC is the photo format your iPhone loves and the rest of the internet politely refuses to deal with. If you’ve ever uploaded a photo and gotten “unsupported format”, you’ve met the HEIC problem.

The fix is simple: convert HEIC to a web-friendly format like WebP. And you can do it without uploading your photos anywhere.

TL;DR

  • Open TinyUtils Image Compressor.
  • Drop your .heic / .heif files.
  • Set output to WebP.
  • Download the converted files (single or ZIP).

Why WebP?

WebP is widely supported in modern browsers and usually produces smaller files than JPEG at similar quality. It’s a great “website default” in 2025.

How to convert HEIC to WebP (no upload)

  1. Go to TinyUtils Image Compressor.
  2. Drag and drop your HEIC files. (Batch is fine — do the whole folder. Live your best life.)
  3. Choose WebP as the output format.
  4. Adjust quality if needed. For photos, starting around 0.80 is usually a solid first try.
  5. Download the results.

Quality tips (so it still looks good)

  • If it’s a photo: WebP around 0.75–0.85 is often the sweet spot.
  • If it’s a screenshot/UI: keep quality higher, or consider PNG/WebP depending on sharp edges.
  • If file size is still large: resizing can have a bigger impact than lowering quality.

FAQ

Is HEIC the same as HEIF?

HEIF is the container format family. HEIC is a common file extension for HEIF images. In practice, treat them the same for conversion.

Should I convert to JPEG instead?

If you need maximum compatibility (older systems, some legacy workflows), JPEG is fine. For web delivery, WebP is usually the better choice.

Does converting require uploading my photos?

With TinyUtils Image Compressor, processing runs in your browser. That means you can convert without sending your photos to a server.
