Why RTF?

  • Universal — Opens on any computer without special software
  • Lightweight — Smaller files for email attachments
  • Editable — Recipients can edit, not just view
  • No macros — Inherently safer than DOCX

How to convert

  1. Go to TinyUtils Document Converter
  2. Upload your .docx file
  3. Choose RTF from the format dropdown
  4. Click Convert
  5. Download and share

What RTF preserves

  • Text formatting (bold, italic, underline)
  • Font styles and sizes
  • Paragraph formatting
  • Basic tables
  • Images (embedded)

Limitations

  • No SmartArt or charts
  • No embedded objects
  • No macros or VBA
  • Complex styles may simplify

When to use RTF

Cross-platform sharing

Sending to Mac, Linux, or older Windows users without Word? RTF ensures they can open and edit.

Email attachments

Some email systems flag DOCX files. RTF passes through without security warnings.

Legacy systems

Older software and systems often support RTF when they can't handle modern DOCX.

FAQ

Is RTF the same quality as DOCX?

RTF preserves content and basic formatting but loses some advanced features. For most documents, you won't notice.

Can I convert back to DOCX?

Yes, but any advanced features lost in the first conversion won't be restored.

Is RTF secure?

RTF can't contain macros, making it safer than DOCX for untrusted documents.
