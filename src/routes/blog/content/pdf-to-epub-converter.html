Understanding PDF and EPUB

What is PDF?

PDF (Portable Document Format) was designed by Adobe to preserve documents exactly—same fonts, same layout, same appearance on any device. PDFs are essentially digital paper. The pages are fixed, dimensions are locked, and content stays precisely where it was placed. This makes PDF perfect for printing and sharing documents that must look identical everywhere.

But fixed layouts are terrible for e-readers. A letter-sized PDF on a 6-inch Kindle screen is nearly unreadable. You can zoom in, but then you're scrolling horizontally on every line. PDF on mobile is even worse. The format simply wasn't designed for adaptive reading experiences.

What is EPUB?

EPUB (Electronic Publication) is the open ebook standard designed specifically for reading. EPUB content reflows to fit any screen—from e-ink readers to tablets to phones. Readers control their experience: font size, typeface, line spacing, margins, color themes. EPUB adapts; PDF doesn't.

EPUB uses web technologies (XHTML and CSS) internally, which is why it can flow like a webpage while functioning as a complete book with chapters, table of contents, and offline access.

Why Convert PDF to EPUB?

1. Readable on Any Screen

PDF pages are fixed size. EPUB text reflows. Convert a PDF to EPUB, and suddenly that content works on a 6-inch Kindle, a 10-inch tablet, or a 4-inch phone—all without zooming or horizontal scrolling.

2. Adjustable Typography

In PDF, the fonts are fixed. In EPUB, you choose the font, size, line height, and margins. Vision difficulties? Increase the text size. Prefer serif fonts? Change it. EPUB gives control to the reader.

3. E-Reader Features

E-readers offer features that only work with reflowable content: dictionary lookup, text-to-speech, highlighting and notes that follow text positions, and reading progress that makes sense. PDF on e-readers is a second-class experience.

4. Comfortable E-Ink Reading

E-ink displays are easier on the eyes than backlit screens, but only if content adapts to them. EPUB renders beautifully on e-ink. PDF on e-ink means shrinking fixed pages to fit tiny screens.

5. Offline Portability

Load EPUBs onto your e-reader and read anywhere—no internet required. Convert PDFs you want to read on planes, trains, or anywhere without connectivity.

What to Expect from Conversion

PDF to EPUB conversion quality varies based on the source PDF:

Works Great For

  • Text-heavy documents: Novels, reports, articles, essays—content that's primarily flowing prose
  • Simple layouts: Single-column text with basic formatting
  • Digital-origin PDFs: PDFs created from Word, Google Docs, or other text sources
  • E-books in PDF form: Books distributed as PDF convert well to their proper ebook format

May Need Attention

  • Multi-column layouts: Columns may merge or reorder unpredictably
  • Complex formatting: Precise positioning, text boxes, and frames may shift
  • Image-heavy content: PDFs with many images may have layout issues
  • Tables and charts: Complex tables may not convert cleanly

Requires OCR First

  • Scanned documents: PDFs made by scanning paper are images, not text. Without OCR (optical character recognition), there's no text to convert. The converter needs text-based PDFs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will my Kindle read EPUB?

Newer Kindles (from 2022 onward) support EPUB natively. For older Kindles, email the EPUB to your Kindle email address (found in device settings)—Amazon converts it to Kindle format automatically. Alternatively, use Calibre to convert EPUB to MOBI or AZW3.

What about page numbers?

EPUB doesn't have fixed pages—that's the point. Text reflows to fit your settings. Instead of page numbers, EPUB uses chapters and progress percentage. This is standard for all e-books.

Can I convert a textbook PDF?

Textbooks with diagrams, multi-column layouts, and complex formatting may not convert cleanly. EPUB works best for flowing prose. Heavily designed textbooks might be better read as PDF with a larger tablet screen.

What about scanned PDFs?

Scanned PDFs contain images of pages, not actual text. The converter needs text data to create an EPUB. For scanned documents, you'd need OCR software first to extract text, then convert.

Will images be included?

Images embedded in the PDF are included in the EPUB. However, images that depend on precise positioning may appear differently in the reflowable format.

What about the table of contents?

If your PDF has a document structure (bookmarks or tagged headings), the converter uses it to create an EPUB table of contents. PDFs without structure get a basic single-chapter EPUB.

What's the maximum file size?

The converter handles PDFs up to 50MB, which covers most documents. Very large PDFs with many images may take longer to process.

Tips for Better Conversions

  • Choose text-based PDFs: Digital-origin PDFs convert better than scanned documents.
  • Simple layouts: Single-column text converts most reliably.
  • Check the result: After conversion, review the EPUB on your e-reader before deleting the PDF.
  • Keep originals: Some PDFs are better read as PDFs. Keep the original if EPUB doesn't work well for that document.

When to Keep PDF

Not all PDFs should be converted:

  • Design-heavy documents: If layout and visual design are critical, PDF preserves them exactly.
  • Reference materials: Technical diagrams, blueprints, and visual references may need fixed layouts.
  • Print preparation: If you're printing, PDF ensures exact output.
  • Forms: PDF forms with fillable fields don't convert to EPUB.
